History

Learn from industry leaders and experienced educators.

Founded in 1969, the university began as a modest technical institute with a bold mission: to make engineering and applied sciences accessible to all. At the heart of this vision was Timothy Stephen M. Lontac, a pioneering educator and systems thinker whose belief in community-driven innovation shaped the school’s earliest programs.

About L.I.G.Y.

Founded in 1969, L.I.G.Y. University has grown into a beacon of inclusive education and technological innovation across Southeast Asia. Our campus is more than just a place of learning — it's a living ecosystem of creativity, collaboration, and excellence.

Nestled in the heart of Quezon City, our facilities include state-of-the-art laboratories, immersive simulation halls, and open-access maker spaces designed for hands-on experimentation. Students gather in our glass-walled library, where natural light meets digital archives, and ideas flow freely across disciplines.

At L.I.G.Y., we believe brilliance thrives in diversity. Our community is built on mutual respect, curiosity, and a shared drive to push boundaries. Whether you're coding in our AI incubator, debating ethics in our amphitheater, or sketching prototypes in the design studio — you're part of a culture that values both rigor and imagination.

We’re not just preparing students for the future — we’re empowering them to shape it. With a faculty of thought leaders and a student body of changemakers, L.I.G.Y. is where gifted minds find their voice, and their purpose.
